What is OCD?

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is a mental illness characterized by recurrent, intrusive thoughts.

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is one of the least recognized and reported disorders in Africa and in Nigeria. However, it is common. OCD is a psychological disorder that is characterized by obsessions and compulsions. An obsession is a recurrent or uncomfortable idea, mental image, or impulse that generates anxiety or emotional distress. A compulsion is an action performed repeatedly in response to an obsession in order to reduce anxiety/stress or to prevent an ugly or fearful event from happening.

Examples of obsessions could include fear of contamination, disturbing mental images, or fear of not locking doors. Compulsions may involve excessive handwashing, repetitive checking, or repeating words to relieve anxiety. While many people have mild obsessive or compulsive behaviors, OCD becomes a disorder when it disrupts daily functioning and causes distress.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition where individuals experience unwanted repetitive thoughts (obsessions) and behaviors (compulsions) they feel they must perform.

Living with OCD can feel overwhelming and exhausting.

Support and treatment can bring real relief and hope.

Key Statistics:

  • 2.3% of the global population will experience OCD at some point in their lives.
  • It often starts during childhood or teenage years, but can begin in adulthood too.
  • Many people live with undiagnosed OCD due to shame or lack of awareness.
  • In Nigeria, mental health awareness is growing, but stigma still delays treatment for many.

Common OCD Symptoms:

  • Fear of germs or contamination
  • Repetitive checking of locks, lights, or appliances
  • Compulsions like counting, touching, or repeating actions
  • Disturbing intrusive thoughts
  • Need for symmetry, order, or perfection

Types of OCD:

  • Contamination: Fear of getting sick or dirty
  • Checking: Worry something bad will happen if not checked
  • Symmetry: Discomfort when things aren’t perfectly arranged
  • Intrusive Thoughts: Harmful or taboo thoughts causing distress

Treatment & Support:

OCD can be effectively managed with the right treatment. This may include:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)
  • Medications (usually SSRIs)
  • Joining a support group or mental health community

If you or someone you know shows signs of OCD, please speak with a mental health professional. Help is available.
